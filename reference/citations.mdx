description: "How to cite the Multitaper Toolbox for academic use, plus the key references for multitaper spectral estimation methodology."
---

If you use this toolbox in published research, you must cite the companion paper listed below. The toolbox implements the methods described in that paper and is maintained by the Prerau Lab at Brigham and Women's Hospital.

## Primary citation

> Prerau MJ, Bianchi MT, Brown RE, Ellenbogen JM, Purdon PL. **Sleep Neurophysiological Dynamics Through the Lens of Multitaper Spectral Analysis.** *Physiology (Bethesda).* 2017 Jan;32(1):60–92. DOI: [10.1152/physiol.00062.2015](https://doi.org/10.1152/physiol.00062.2015). PubMed PMID: 27927806.

```bibtex
@article{prerau2017sleep,
  author  = {Prerau, Michael J. and Bianchi, Matt T. and Brown, Ritchie E.
             and Ellenbogen, Jeffrey M. and Purdon, Patrick L.},
  title   = {Sleep Neurophysiological Dynamics Through the Lens of
             Multitaper Spectral Analysis},
  journal = {Physiology (Bethesda)},
  year    = {2017},
  volume  = {32},
  number  = {1},
  pages   = {60--92},
  doi     = {10.1152/physiol.00062.2015},
  pmid    = {27927806}
}
```

## Methodological references

1. **Thomson DJ.** Spectrum estimation and harmonic analysis. *Proc IEEE* 70:1055–1096, 1982. *(Original multitaper paper)*

2. **Bronez T.** On the performance advantage of multitaper spectral analysis. *IEEE Trans Signal Proc* 40:2941–2946, 1992.

3. **Percival DB, Walden AT.** *Spectral Analysis for Physical Applications: Multitaper and Conventional Univariate Techniques.* Cambridge University Press, 1993. *(Source for the adaptive weighting algorithm, pp. 368–370)*
